## HuaShu (花叔)

**AI Native Coder · Indie Developer**

HuaShu — known online as 花叔 ("Uncle Hua") — is an AI content creator and indie developer with over **300,000 followers** across platforms. He is best known for building fully functional, commercially successful software products entirely with AI tools, without writing a single line of code manually.

That last part is not a figure of speech. Every product HuaShu has shipped — including an **App Store #1 Paid iOS app** — was built by working with AI coding agents and prompt engineering, not by writing code directly. His work is a live proof of concept for a new kind of developer: one whose core skill is knowing how to work with AI, not how to write syntax.

---

## What HuaShu does

HuaShu creates practical, accessible content about AI tools — documentation, tutorials, and free guides that explain how to actually use the tools practitioners are talking about, not just what those tools are. His 橙皮书 (Orange Book) series distills that practical knowledge into structured guides that anyone can follow.

His work is grounded in direct experience. Every book in the series covers tools he has used to build real things. When he writes about Hermes Agent's self-improving learning loop or Claude Code's session model, he is drawing on hands-on knowledge from building with those tools — not summarizing documentation.

The audience he writes for is developers and AI enthusiasts who learn best by doing: people who want to understand how a tool works well enough to actually use it, not just to describe it.

## A note on method

HuaShu's approach to AI-assisted development is itself worth understanding, because it shapes how he writes about AI tools.

He does not treat AI agents as autocomplete for developers who already know what they are doing. He treats them as a different kind of creative partner — one where the quality of the outcome depends less on syntax knowledge and more on clarity of intent, feedback quality, and understanding of the tool's strengths and failure modes.

This perspective informs every Orange Book. The guides are written for people who want to work *with* AI tools effectively, which requires a different kind of understanding than traditional programming documentation provides.
