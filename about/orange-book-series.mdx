## What is the 橙皮书 series?

橙皮书 — pronounced *chéng pí shū*, literally "orange skin book" — is a series of free, practical guides on AI tools written by [HuaShu (花叔)](/about/author).

The series has a simple mission: **make cutting-edge AI tools accessible and practical**. Not accessible in the sense of dumbed-down, but accessible in the sense of genuinely usable — the kind of understanding that lets you actually build things with the tool, not just describe what it does.

Each book in the series covers a specific tool or concept in depth. The format is consistent: grounded in direct hands-on experience, structured for learning rather than reference, and free to download without any paywall or registration.

## The series

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Claude Code: From Beginner to Expert" icon="terminal">
    A complete guide to Claude Code — Anthropic's CLI-based coding agent. Covers setup, effective prompting, session management, and advanced workflows for developers who want to get the most out of Claude's coding capabilities.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Harness Engineering" icon="sliders">
    The conceptual foundation for the series. Covers the five components of effective AI agent design: instructions, constraints, feedback, memory, and orchestration. Understanding Harness Engineering makes every other book in the series easier to read.
  </Card>
  <Card title="OpenClaw" icon="code-branch">
    A practical guide to OpenClaw, the open-source, model-agnostic agent framework. Covers configuration, tool integration, workflow composition, and the tradeoffs involved in running a highly extensible agent platform.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Hermes Agent: The Complete Guide" icon="brain">
    The book you are reading now. Covers Hermes Agent v0.7.0 — the self-improving, memory-equipped agent from Nous Research. Seventeen chapters across five parts, from concepts to real-world scenarios to deep thinking about where self-improving agents are heading.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
